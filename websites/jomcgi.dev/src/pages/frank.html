Hiking Trails

🥾 Dog Mountain

Easy 5km round trip from Mount Seymour parking lot with spectacular 360° panoramic views—North Vancouver, Lions Gate Bridge, downtown skyline, Mount Baker, and Fraser Valley. Moderate difficulty despite minimal elevation gain due to slippery roots and rocky sections.

🥾 St. Marks Summit

10.5km round trip with 567m elevation gain. Exceptional North Shore views of Howe Sound, all its islands, Tantalus Range glaciers, and the Lions. First half well-maintained gravel, second half technical with steep switchbacks. Best July-November. 4-5 hours return.

🥾 Tunnel Bluffs

11.5km with 470m elevation gain to spectacular clifftop viewpoint with 180-degree views of Howe Sound at 686m elevation. Access via Sunset Drive trailhead in Lions Bay (old Tunnel Point trailhead no longer accessible). Limited parking—arrive early or weekdays. 4 hours return.

Icefields Parkway

🏔️ About the Parkway

232km of world-class scenery connecting Jasper and Banff through the Canadian Rockies. Limited services (only one gas station), no cell coverage. Requires Parks Canada pass ($11/person day pass or $151.25 annual Discovery Pass). Download offline maps and fill gas before departure.

Major Stops

🏔️ Peyto Lake

Most photographed lake in the Canadian Rockies. Unique turquoise color from glacial rock flour and distinctive wolf-head shape from viewpoint. 10-15 minute wheelchair-accessible walk from Bow Summit parking.

Best times: Early morning before 9am (serene, fewer crowds) or late afternoon after 4pm (warm glow). Lake colors most vibrant late morning to mid-afternoon (late June-early September) when sun is higher.

💦 Athabasca Falls

Most powerful waterfall on the Parkway. Not the tallest (23m), but incredible force and volume as river narrows through limestone gorge. Fed by Columbia Icefield. Wheelchair-accessible paved pathway.

Best times: Early morning before 8am or late afternoon/evening after 7pm. Most spectacular flow late May through June due to glacial snowmelt.

❄️ Columbia Icefield / Athabasca Glacier

Largest mass of glacial ice in the Canadian Rockies. Ice Explorer tours take you onto 10,000-year-old glacier. Includes Glacier Skywalk—glass-floored platform 280m above Sunwapta Valley.

Season: May 3-October 13, 2025. Adult $116, Youth $76. Book advance at banffjaspercollection.com. 20% off after 3:30pm.

💙 Bow Lake

Vibrant turquoise glacial lake with Crowfoot Glacier backdrop. Accessible right from highway with shoreline access at Num-Ti-Jah Lodge.

Best times: Early morning at sunrise for mirror reflections with soft light, or late afternoon/evening at sunset for warm golden tones. Calm conditions essential—avoid midday on windy days.

💦 Sunwapta Falls

Upper Falls: 100m wheelchair-accessible walk, 18.5m powerful drop through narrow gorge. Lower Falls: 1.3km downstream, less crowded, intimate canyon with three smaller cascades.

Best times: Early morning before 8am or late afternoon after 7pm. Most spectacular flow late spring/early summer (May-June) from glacial melt.

🏞️ Mistaya Canyon

Dramatic limestone canyon with narrow twisting gorge carved by Mistaya River. Multiple cascades through carved limestone. Wooden footbridge provides views straight down into gorge. Quick 25-40 minute total commitment.

Best times: Early morning before 9am or late afternoon after 7pm for peaceful experience. Avoid midday crowds.

Wildlife Viewing

🦌 What to Look For

  • Bighorn Sheep: Sandy coat, white rump, massive curved horns on rams. Often at Tangle Falls, Medicine Lake, higher viewpoints.
  • Mountain Goats: White coats, short black horns, beards. Best at Kerkeslin Goat Lick (38km south of Jasper).
  • Elk: Most commonly seen. Tan body, dark neck, creamy rump. DANGEROUS during calving (May-June) and rutting (Aug-Sept) seasons.
  • Grizzly Bears: Large shoulder hump, dish-shaped face. Bow Valley Parkway in spring/early summer.
  • Black Bears: Straight face profile, larger ears, no shoulder hump. Various colors despite name.

⏰ Best Viewing Times

Dawn and dusk are optimal—animals most active during feeding hours. Midday offers poorest viewing. Summer sunrise 5:30-7am, sunset around 9:30-9:40pm.

Spring (Apr-Jun): Best for bears foraging after hibernation. Fall (Sep-Oct): Elk rutting season with bugling, bighorn sheep mating battles. Summer: Grizzlies move higher (harder to see from roads).
